Computer Science
FAB 120, 503.725.4036

Students who are intending to graduate with an undergraduate degree in Computer Science must be admitted to the Computer Science program prior to taking their last 48 credits of upper division Computer Science courses.

Students who do not have the formal pre-requisites for a class need instructor's permission to enroll. Students who are not admitted to the department and who want to take upper division classes need department permission to enroll.

Curious about computer science? Want to learn to code, but aren’t sure if computing is the right major or minor for you? Check out our new special topics class, CS 199.

CS 199, Spst: Prog & Prob Solv: A Frst Exp is an introduction to programming and computer science for students with no prior programming experience. It is designed to be a CS 161 alternative for students who: are not computer science majors, might benefit from a more accessible pace, want to explore programming but are unsure about pursing a computer science major/minor, and/or do not have a strong math or computing background.
